terms of entry

Being a large visitor attraction, there is always lots going on at paignton zoo!

Here is where we let you know about all of the latest updates prior to your visit, including any exhibit
closures, weather updates, animals off-show or planned maintenance work.

We always aim to offer the best possible experience for our guests and thank you for your understanding.


BOARDWALK CLOSURE

Our boardwalk is being redeveloped, so when you visit the zoo from now on your entry and exit route
will be slightly different. Instead of going across the boardwalk you will be directed through Brookside
Aviary and then follow the signposted path around into the zoo. When you exit the zoo follow the
big cats route back to Brookside Aviary. As you are probably aware, there is a nationwide
shortage of building materials, and we are working with our contractors to start this project as soon
as possible. We will keep this page updated on
how our boardwalk redevelopment is progressing.


PATH CLOSURE

The path between the mandrills and rhinos will be closed throughout November to allow essential maintenance
to take place on the rhino wall. We apologise for any inconvenience that this may cause.


TICKETING AND ENTRY

Pre-booking online is not essential, however, to ensure that you are able to visit Paignton Zoo on your
preferred date and time, we would suggest booking your tickets in advance. Annual Pass holders may check in
using Gate 5.

Face coverings and social distancing are no longer compulsory in indoor or outdoor areas. However, please do
respect guests and team members who still wish to adhere to these measures.

If you have a complimentary ticket, existing pre-paid ticket, Childminder Pass or Welcome Return ticket,
please call 01803 697500 to book your time slot as we cannot process these bookings online.

For more details, please see our Staying Safe at the Zoo guide here.

Please note: we cannot transfer any ticket or time slot bookings.


WHICH EXHIBITS ARE OPEN/CLOSED AND ANIMALS OFF SHOW

All outdoor exhibits and the majority of our indoor exhibits are open. Our indoor soft play is now open.
Face coverings are no longer required in indoor spaces.

The following exhibits are currently closed due to maintenance: Giraffe House (outdoor viewing available),
Jungle Express Train and Nocturnal House.

There may be times when some of our animals may not be viewable at the exact time that you visit an exhibit
as we not do not ‘force’ them to be ‘on-show’. If an animal is not immediately visible, they could be
resting in their off-show area or somewhere out of view in their naturalistic exhibit, so please make time
to pop back later in the day – it will be worth the wait!

For example, our big cats are usually more active and easier to spot in the afternoons. We cannot guarantee
that you will see every single animal of the day of your visit.


FOOD & DRINK

We are serving a full food and drink menu every day at Paignton Zoo.
The Island Restaurant is open daily from 10am until half an hour before the zoo closes and other catering
cabins around the site are also open (serving times do vary).
Vegan, vegetarian and gluten-free options are available in all of our food outlets, with plenty of outdoor
seating and picnic areas for guests to use.

If you have a query that is not answered here, please email us at paigntonzoo@wildplanettrust.org.uk and we
will aim to get back to you as quickly as possible.
And to further keep up-to-date with everything Paignton Zoo related, please make sure you follow our social
media channels too!
